# Strategy Review

## Sharpened Mission

Become the trusted AI operating layer for financial reporting teams, turning fragmented systems and institutional knowledge into audit-ready answers, workflows, and evidence.

The product should not start as a broad autonomous agent platform. It should start as the place reporting teams trust to answer:

- Where did this number come from?
- Why did it change?
- What support exists?
- What did we do last period?
- Can we defend this during review or audit?

## Wedge

Start with audit-ready reporting intelligence.

The first wedge is source-backed answers and artifacts across reporting files, ASC/SEC guidance, company policies, prior filings, and support workbooks.

The strongest first workflows are:

- SCF support generation and evidence package.
- Reporting question answering with citations.
- Disclosure support and tie-out.
- Prior-period reporting memory.
- Audit support package preparation.

## Target Buyer And Users

Primary buyer:

- Controller.
- Chief Accounting Officer.
- VP Finance.
- Director of Financial Reporting.

Primary users:

- SEC reporting teams.
- Financial reporting managers.
- Technical accounting teams.
- Audit support teams.
- Disclosure teams.

Best early ICP:

- Public or late-stage pre-IPO companies.
- 5-50 person accounting/reporting teams.
- Multi-entity or multi-system reporting environments.
- Workiva-heavy or Excel-heavy reporting processes.
- Recurring SEC, audit, disclosure, and tie-out burden.

## Moat

The moat is not generic AI agents. The moat is reporting trust infrastructure.

Defensible assets:

- Reporting-specific knowledge graph connecting numbers, entities, accounts, policies, prior disclosures, assumptions, preparers, reviewers, and evidence.
- Institutional memory of prior decisions, thresholds, adjustments, and review rationale.
- Audit-grade provenance for every answer and artifact.
- Workflow data exhaust from recurring reporting cycles.
- Governed trust layer for AI actions in regulated finance environments.

## Strategic Non-Goals

Do not become, at least in the early product:

- A new ERP.
- A Workiva replacement.
- A general finance chatbot.
- A full close-management platform.
- A BI/dashboard product.
- A filing system of record.
- An autonomous accounting agent without human review.
- A horizontal enterprise knowledge management product.

## 90-Day Priorities

1. Pick one painful beachhead workflow.
   Recommendation: trusted source-backed reporting Q&A plus SCF/disclosure support artifacts.

2. Run customer discovery.
   Interview Controllers, CAOs, SEC reporting managers, and audit support leads. Validate buying trigger, willingness to pay, required integrations, and trust requirements.

3. Build a defensible sourced-answer prototype.
   It should ingest reporting packages, support files, policies, Excel workbooks, and accounting guidance, then return citations, source lineage, missing support, and reviewer-ready explanations.

4. Create trust architecture early.
   Design for permissions, evidence, version history, approval state, audit logs, and refusal when support is missing.

5. Prove cycle-time ROI.
   Measure time saved on support gathering, tie-outs, flux explanations, disclosure drafting, and audit request responses.
